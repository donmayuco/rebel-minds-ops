Closing line under any treatment: “If your business runs on something else, that’s fine. We integrate; you own the keys.”

Treatment 1 groups tools by job but reads as a finite menu. Treatment 2 keeps carousel recognizability (brand dots) but same finite-set problem. Treatment 3 fixes the semantics: cropped edges imply the list continues past the frame, “recent builds have touched” frames it as evidence rather than catalog, and the open-ended footer line says anything-and-everything outright. The three are composable: 3’s framing copy + cropped strip can carry 2’s brand-color dots.

How to read these: A is the critique’s recommendation (maximum distance from the anti-reference, daylight register matches the operator personality). B is the risk-taker (structure as identity; nobody else’s homepage is a ledger). C is the conservative move (keeps your cyan-dark equity, fixes the tropes, not the lane). All three keep the type system you already chose for rebelminds.ai, so principle 6 (“one family, two voices”) holds in every case. Mix-and-match is fair game: for example, A’s daylight body with B’s ledger as the proof section.
